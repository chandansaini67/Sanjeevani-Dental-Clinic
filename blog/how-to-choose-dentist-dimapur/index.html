How to Choose the Best Dentist in Dimapur

Dr. Deepika Jain Written by Dr. Deepika Jain (Dental Surgeon) · 5 July 2026

Choosing a dentist is really about choosing who you trust with your health and your money. Whether you're new to Dimapur or just fed up with your current clinic, here's a practical checklist — from people who do this every day.

1. Hygiene you can see

The single most important thing. Instruments should be sterilized in an autoclave between every patient, and disposables (needles, gloves, suction tips) should be opened fresh in front of you. If you can't tell whether tools are sterilized, ask — a good clinic is proud to show you.

2. Honest, upfront pricing

A trustworthy clinic tells you the cost before treatment, in writing, and explains what it covers. Be cautious of quotes that balloon halfway through. This is exactly why we publish our price ranges openly and give a written estimate after examination.

3. The right specialist for the job

General dentistry, oral surgery and orthodontics are different skills. A clinic with a team of specialists — like a dental surgeon for fillings, an oral surgeon for difficult extractions, and an orthodontist for braces — means you're treated by someone who does that procedure often.

4. Real reviews from real patients

Read the Google reviews, and look for specifics: names of treatments, comments about pain being managed, staff being kind. A steady stream of detailed 4–5 star reviews is a better signal than a single perfect score. You can read ours here.

5. They listen before they drill

A good dentist examines, explains what's wrong, lays out your options (including doing nothing), and lets you decide. If you feel rushed into an expensive plan on the first visit, that's a red flag.

Warning signs to avoid

  • Reused or visibly unclean instruments
  • Prices that change without explanation
  • Pressure to start expensive treatment immediately
  • No willingness to answer your questions
  • "Painless" promises with no mention of anaesthesia or aftercare

A simple test

Book a consultation for a check-up (₹200–₹500 at most clinics here) and notice how you're treated. Are your questions welcomed? Is the place clean? Do the costs make sense? Your gut plus this checklist will tell you a lot.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know if a dental clinic is hygienic?

Instruments should come from a sterilization pouch, disposables opened fresh in front of you, and surfaces wiped between patients. A good clinic happily explains its sterilization process when asked.

Should I choose the cheapest dentist?

Choose the one with honest, upfront pricing and good hygiene — not simply the lowest quote. Cut-price dentistry that skips sterilization or uses poor materials costs far more to fix later.

Is it worth travelling to a specialist clinic?

For complex work — surgery, braces, implants — yes. Being treated by a dentist who performs that procedure regularly gives better, safer results than a one-size-fits-all approach.
