Signs you're due for a cleaning

  • Bleeding gums when you brush
  • Persistent bad breath
  • Yellow or brown tartar near the gum line
  • Tea, coffee or tobacco stains
  • It's been more than 6 months since your last visit
  • Gums that look swollen or feel tender

What happens, step by step

  1. Examination

    The doctor checks your teeth and gums, with a digital X-ray only if something needs a closer look.

  2. Ultrasonic scaling

    A fine vibrating tip loosens tartar from teeth and below the gum line — no cutting, no drilling.

  3. Polishing

    A soft rotating cup with polishing paste smooths the surfaces so plaque struggles to stick again.

  4. Advice & plan

    You get honest guidance on brushing, and a written plan if anything else needs attention.

Common questions

Does scaling loosen teeth?

No — it removes the tartar that was damaging the support of your teeth. Any 'loose' feeling afterwards is the absence of hardened deposits, and it settles quickly.

How often should I get my teeth cleaned?

Every six months for most people. If you have gum disease, smoke, or build tartar quickly, your dentist may suggest every 3–4 months.

Will cleaning whiten my teeth?

It removes surface stains, so teeth look noticeably fresher — but it does not change the shade of the enamel itself. For that, see our teeth whitening treatment.

Is the check-up really necessary if nothing hurts?

Yes — cavities and gum disease are painless in their early, cheap-to-fix stages. A ₹200–500 check-up regularly saves patients thousands later.
