Treat these as emergencies

Severe toothache that painkillers barely touch
Swelling of the gum, cheek or face
A tooth knocked out or broken in an accident
Bleeding that doesn't stop after an extraction
A lost filling or crown with sharp pain
Pus or a bad-tasting discharge near a tooth

Do ✓

  • Rinse gently with warm salt water
  • Cold compress outside the cheek for swelling
  • For a knocked-out adult tooth: hold it by the crown, keep it in milk, come within 60 minutes
  • Take paracetamol as per the packet if needed

Don't ✗

  • Place aspirin on the gum — it burns tissue
  • Apply heat to a swollen face
  • Scrub or scrape a knocked-out tooth's root
  • Wait days hoping severe pain will pass — it spreads

Aftercare

  • Follow the written instructions we give before you leave
  • Complete any prescribed antibiotics fully
  • Return for the definitive treatment — emergency relief is step one

Common questions

What if the emergency happens on a Sunday or at night?

Message us on WhatsApp anyway — we'll reply with first-aid guidance as soon as possible and book you the first slot when the clinic opens at 9 AM.

A tooth got knocked out — can it be saved?

Often yes, if you act fast: hold it by the crown (not the root), keep it in milk or inside the cheek, and reach us within an hour. Minutes matter.

How fast can you see me?

Message first and come during clinic hours — we keep same-day emergency slots Monday to Saturday and prioritize genuine pain cases.

Will emergency treatment be expensive?

The emergency visit is ₹300–₹800. Any treatment needed after that is quoted in writing before we begin — no surprise bills, even in a crisis.
