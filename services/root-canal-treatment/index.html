Root Canal Treatment (RCT) in Dimapur

A root canal treatment (RCT) removes infected pulp from inside a tooth, cleans and seals the canals, and saves the natural tooth instead of pulling it out. At Sanjeevani Dental Clinic in Dimapur, RCT costs ₹3,000–₹6,000 per tooth and is painless with modern anaesthesia — often finished in a single sitting.

Signs you may need a root canal

  • Severe toothache while chewing
  • Pain that wakes you up at night
  • Long-lasting sensitivity to hot or cold
  • A darkened or grey tooth
  • Swelling or a pimple-like bump on the gum
  • A deep cavity reaching the nerve

What happens, step by step

  1. Diagnosis & X-ray

    A digital X-ray shows how deep the infection goes and confirms RCT is the right choice — not every ache needs one.

  2. Numbing

    Local anaesthesia makes the tooth completely numb. You feel pressure, not pain.

  3. Cleaning the canals

    The infected pulp is removed and each canal is cleaned, shaped and disinfected.

  4. Filling & sealing

    Canals are sealed with a rubber-like material (gutta-percha) so bacteria cannot return.

  5. Crown protection

    A crown is usually advised afterwards to make the treated tooth strong for chewing — priced separately.

Aftercare

  • Mild soreness for 1–2 days — a normal painkiller handles it
  • Chew on the other side until the crown is placed
  • Get the crown within a few weeks for best protection
  • Return immediately if swelling increases

Common questions

Does a root canal hurt?

With modern anaesthesia, most patients feel little to nothing during the procedure — many say it felt like a long filling. The pain people fear is usually the infection before treatment, not the treatment itself.

How many visits will I need?

Many of our root canals finish in a single sitting. Badly infected teeth may need two visits so medicine can work inside the tooth between them.

Is extraction cheaper than RCT?

Pulling the tooth costs less today, but the gap it leaves usually needs a bridge or implant later — costing far more. Saving your natural tooth is almost always the better investment.

What happens if I delay?

The infection can spread to the bone, turn into a painful abscess, and make the tooth unsavable. Early treatment is cheaper, faster and more comfortable.
