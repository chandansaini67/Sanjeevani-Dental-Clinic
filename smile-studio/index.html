Your teeth, and what each one does

Your upper arch has incisors (the flat front teeth that bite), canines (the pointed corner teeth that tear), premolars and molars (the broad back teeth that grind). Different teeth face different problems:

  • Front teeth — chips, gaps and staining, helped by whitening and braces.
  • Back teeth — deep cavities and cracks, treated with root canals and crowns.
  • Wisdom molars — often impacted, needing extraction.

How a tooth is saved: the 4 stages

  1. Healthy tooth

    Hard enamel protects the living pulp inside.

  2. Cavity forms

    Decay breaks through the enamel toward the nerve, causing pain.

  3. Root canal

    The infected pulp is removed and the canals are cleaned and sealed.

  4. Crown fitted

    A crown protects the treated tooth so it works like new. See root canal treatment.

How braces straighten teeth

Braces and clear aligners apply gentle, constant pressure that gradually moves crooked or crowded teeth into a straight, healthy arch over 12–24 months. The result is not just a better-looking smile — straight teeth are easier to clean and less prone to decay. Explore braces & aligners.
